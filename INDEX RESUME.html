Complete Graph (2 Marks): A Complete Graph in graph theory is a type of graph in which every pair of distinct vertices is connected by a unique edge. In other words, there is an edge between every two vertices in the graph. - Properties:   - If there are n vertices, a complete graph has n(n-1)/2 edges.   - All vertices have a degree of n-1, meaning every vertex is connected to every other vertex. Example: For 4 vertices (A, B, C, D), a complete graph will have edges: AB, AC, AD, BC, BD, CD.
